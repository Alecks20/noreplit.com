Over the past few months, Replit.com has changed their pricing drastically to the point where it is simply not viable.
We, along with many others, have used Replit for years- however, at its current point we are all looking for alternatives.
This website covers what happened, a comparison, alternatives, and community quotes to help you transition away from the platform.

What happened?

Nerfing Hacker Plan

The cutback of "Always On" instances from five to one hit Hacker Plan users hard, who used to enjoy five continuous repls but now are limited to just one. This shift likely disrupted many users' workflows and slowed down project progress.

Similarly, reducing boosts from five to one negatively affected the user experience. While Replit aimed to align resources with costs, this change might have eroded the Hacker Plan's appeal, causing dissatisfaction among its users.

Outrageous AI

Replit's enduring focus on AI and LLMs have put them in a state of throwing money at a losing battle. Although Ghostwriter is $10/month, it uses the same technology one can get for extraordinarily less from OpenAI.

Trending Repls have been focused on AI, as well as the company's social media accounts & most heavily, their CEO.

10x Hike in Power-ups

Replit's price hike for power-ups like Boosts and the Always On feature greatly affected its mostly student and budding coder user base. The increased costs, from 2 to 70 cycles/day for a Boost and 2 to 20 cycles/day for the Always On feature, made these features less accessible, impacting the learning community.

The community backlash was notable, with many finding the new pricing off-putting. This led some to abandon the Hacker Plan or even Replit, as the Hacker Plan's affordability, a key attraction, seemed compromised.

Actually laughable "Reserved VMs"

The first offender of their Deployments is Reserved VMs. For the lowest tier, you get 0.25vCPUs, 1GiB of RAM, and 10GiB of outbound data transfer.

On platforms such as DigitalOcean and AWS, they can be more than 70% cheaper.

Intentionally misleading "Autoscale"

Replit's version of a worker-like VM is Autoscale. You get 3 MILLION free compute "units" per month with Hacker.

If you're confused by what that means, everyone else is too. Replit's site says that most projects will use under 20 cents a month, however with a DDoS bypass or a "viral Repl", you can easily incur costs upwards of hundreds of dollars.

On their pricing page, if you had the Lowest Tier Autoscale running for a full month with constant load, you would incur ~$64/month.

Uncompetitive Static

Replit's static deployments is just that- static site hosting. Just one small issue, and pay attention- all of these sites are free.

Egress Limits

On April 7, 2023, Replit set egress limits across all plans. Aimed at controlling outbound data transfer, this move faced backlash, especially from the Free tier users.

With limits set at 10 GiB for Free, 50 GiB for Hacker, and 100 GiB for Pro tiers, many found them restrictive, disrupting workflows, particularly for those using free resources for learning and small projects.

Replit.dev

The final blow came when Replit announced that repls could only be run within the IDE, eliminating the option to share them outside - now, Replit Deployments stood as the sole avenue.

This decision seemed to counter Replit's user-friendly ethos, sparking widespread confusion and shock within the community.

The announcement led to a significant backlash, drama, and a series of bans.

Misleading Bounties

The top 50 most "rewarding" open bounties are all stale, having been left open for months with no accepted applicant. Replit still shows this on their "60k+ OPEN" tag, show to every user, including students.

The payout is shown in USD and Cycles, but doesn't take into account the 25%-15% cashout fee.

Terrible community management

The 16k+ member Replit Discord community fell to bits after the moderation team wasn't paid any more than their Hacker plan, all nerfed and shiny at $7/month. Understandably, moderators striked, causing Replit to abandon the community altogether.

Replit's Reps program was abandoned too, with absolutely no talk of them past the 1st cohort.

Jams were reduced from events like Kajam and #madewithreplit to AI hackathons that a 14 year old won with school & no prior experience. Prize pools have dropped from over $10k to around $2k per event, lowering the incentive.

Disrespect.

Alternatives

Completely Free

  1. Glitch

    • Description: Glitch is an online coding platform that emphasizes creative and collaborative coding. It allows you to create and remix projects, experiment with coding, and collaborate with others in real time. It's particularly suitable for building interactive web applications.

  2. Goorm, Credit: python660

    • Description: Goorm is a cloud-based development platform originating in South Korea that offers a robust set of features. It provides flexibility in choosing programming languages, database options, and Ubuntu OS versions. With a generous amount of storage and a daily traffic limit of 1GB, it is suitable for both small and large projects. The platform also supports both public and private projects.

  3. CodeSandbox

    • Description: CodeSandbox is an online development environment tailored for web applications. It offers a convenient way to create, edit, and test React, Vue, and Angular projects in a sandboxed environment, making it easy to prototype and share code.

  4. StackBlitz

    • Description: StackBlitz offers an online IDE for web development with features like real-time collaboration and instant dependency searches. It's tailored for front-end development and supports popular web frameworks like Angular, React, and Vue.js.

  5. Hop

    • Description: Hop provides an online coding platform that allows you to write, compile, and run code in various programming languages. It offers a user-friendly interface for programming using VSCode.

  6. GitHub Codespaces

    • Description: GitHub Codespaces integrates with GitHub repositories, enabling you to create and customize development environments for your projects directly in your browser. This is particularly useful for collaborative coding and maintaining consistent development environments across team members.

  7. Gitpod

    • Description: Gitpod offers ready-to-code development environments for GitHub projects, allowing you to start coding immediately without any setup.

  8. Cyclic.sh, Credit: python660

    • Description: Cyclic.sh is a specialized platform for hosting and running scheduled jobs or periodic tasks. The service focuses on ease of use and scalability, allowing you to set up, run, and monitor recurring tasks without the hassle of managing servers or cron jobs.

  9. Vercel

    • Description: Vercel is a cloud platform that specializes in deploying and hosting web applications. It's known for its speed and ease of use, supporting various frameworks and offering features like serverless functions and automatic scaling.

  10. Netlify

    • Description: Netlify is a platform focused on web development and deployment. It offers features like continuous integration, automated deployments, and hosting for static sites and web applications. It simplifies the process of deploying and managing web projects.

  11. Cloudflare Pages

    • Description: Cloudflare Pages is a free way to deploy static sites. It's great for production-level deployment, providing you with a [project].pages.dev URL, along with support for custom domains and more.

  12. Static

    • Description: Static is a platform that offers simple and fast deployment of static websites with a focus on performance and security.

  13. Render

    • Description: Render is a unified platform to build and run all your apps and websites with free SSL, a global CDN, private networks, and auto deploys from Git.

  14. Coder

    • Description: Coder provides an enterprise-grade development environment that allows developers to code from anywhere.
